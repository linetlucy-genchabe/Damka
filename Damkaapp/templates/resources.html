Useful information

Sickle cell disease (SCD) is the most common hemoglobinopathy encountered in Kenya. In the western part of the country, about 4.5% of children are born with SCD, and 18% of children are born with sickle cell trait. SCD has contributed significantly to the mortality rate in children younger than age 5 years, primarily because of late diagnosis, educational gaps among service providers, and lack of access to appropriate treatment.
